Background

Source & Purpose

Povo is an app created during a UX design foundation course. The primary learning objectives in the course were to lay the foundations of good UX design principles and heuristics prior to undertaking a more holistic and intensive course in UX Design.

The app has been designed to assist users in increase their vocabulary in their given topic of interest in a more casual and stressfree format.

Problem Statement

With the ever growing expectations of modern day society on hightened intellect for even the most basic jobs, more and more people are feeling the pressure to further educate themselves so as to progress in their fields and in every day life. With many individuals working more than one job or having to study alongside their full time jobs, it leaves little time and cognative capacity left to learn the new vocabulary they need to propel them forwards. Additionally, the common methods of learning new vocabulary can be mentally draining and relatively dated when it comes to learning efficiency.

Project Goals

  • To deliver a personalised educational tool.
  • To assist indviduals in furthuring their vocabulary while minimising the percieved effort to do so.
  • To design a simplistic and easy to use app that users will want to return to.

Key Focus Areas

  • What are the main motivations and frustrations people have when trying to learn new vocabulary and topics?
  • What method of consuming informative content do individuals enjoy using the most?
  • How do people fit learning new vocabulary into their every day lives?

Persona

A persona was needed for this project so as to ensure that the designs were addressing real issues the end users face. The persona was created using data that was collected and analysed from the initial interviews with potential future users. To show the importance of both the interviews and persona, it was observed that one of the behavious mentioned by multible interviewees was regarding their use of podcast and audio books. This find resulted in the app's main feature revolving around the use of audio podcast format as opposed to more traditional froms of learning.

User Flows

Initial user flows were created based on how it was beieved that the persona navigate through the app. Although this greatly helped withh the initial app architecture, it was later altered after conducting usability tests with potential users. Over time the app architecture will undergo constant improvements as new insights are discovered.

Low Fidelity Wireframes

Several highly iterative low fidelity wireframe session with set time restrictions were conducted to produce a variety of layout options for the app. After each session the wireframes were assessed and the best features were taken forward into later session. The wireframes continued to evolve through the project as new insights were uncovered. The wireframes displayed are of the "Onboarding" journey, however, they were later altered for a more simple and direct message which you can see if you click through to the high fidelity prototype at the bottom of this page.

High Fidelity Wireframes

Low fidelity prototypes were created and subject to a usability test. Although majority of the test analysis came back positive, there were a number of users who failed to view all of the onboarding steps prior to loging into the app. Athought this is not a catastrophic issue, it would be wise to assume that users who skip the onboarding process will not utilize the product to its full potential. This could then result in a high churn rate. To overcome this the onboarding process was imptoved in later wireframes where the option for signing up is placed after the onboarding steps have been completed. These can be seen if you click through to the high fidelity prototype at the bottom of this page.
